## Official Baseline

The official-source baseline keeps native DAT phone apps separate from display
Web Apps. DAT is the native path for camera photo/video capture and native
display lifecycle when package, DAM, firmware, glasses app, and release-channel
requirements are met. Microphone and speaker/headphone behavior is modeled as
phone OS Bluetooth audio route state, not raw DAT audio packets. Display Web
Apps are the documented browser-style path for display rendering, Meta Neural
Band input, captouch, motion/orientation, phone GPS, and local storage.

No checked official source exposes raw Bluetooth or Wi-Fi sockets, raw Meta
Neural Band EMG, Web Apps camera capture, Web Apps microphone capture, or direct
speaker/headphone route control. Therefore IPFS, libp2p, and MCP++ compatibility
is a Swissknife/Hallucinate App bridge-envelope property above Meta transport
surfaces.

## Contract And Mocks

The Swissknife capability contract requires these capability families:
`camera.photo_capture`, `camera.video_capture`, `microphone.input`,
`speaker.output`, `headphone.output`, `display.output`, `neural_band.input`,
`captouch.input`, `motion.orientation`, and `phone_gps.context`.

The hardware-free fixture covers ready states, app binding IDs, permission
scopes, sample payload references, control-plane envelopes, and failure modes.
It explicitly declares `credentials_required=false`,
`dat_package_access_required=false`, `paired_glasses_required=false`, and
`physical_hardware_required=false`, so it is valid for CI and Playwright but not
for final physical-device signoff.

## Bridge Envelope

Bluetooth and Wi-Fi are route labels only. The bridge envelope requires device
and session identity, app binding ID, permission state, latency/backpressure,
payload limits, content CIDs, policy decisions, privacy redaction metadata,
control-plane route decisions, and MCP++ receipts. The invariant
`raw_transport_is_ipfs_libp2p_or_mcp=false` prevents raw radio routes from being
misrepresented as IPFS/libp2p/MCP++ transports.

Wi-Fi handoff can carry libp2p peer/session IDs only when the phone app,
display-webapp, or simulator provides that app layer. Bluetooth audio routes can
carry content CIDs and MCP++ receipts generated by the app bridge, but they must
not claim libp2p identity unless the bridge declares libp2p support.

## App Bindings And Control Plane

Swissknife apps bind Meta glasses I/O through stable app binding IDs. Camera
routes use capture requests and content-addressed refs. Microphone and
speaker/headphone routes publish Bluetooth audio state and optional transcript
or audio artifact refs. Display routes publish content-addressed widget/action
refs. Meta Neural Band, captouch, motion/orientation, and phone GPS routes
publish normalized event or context descriptors.

The control-plane router maps those bindings to ORB/MCP++ tool calls,
Hallucinate App policy handoff, app session state, replay protection,
backpressure, privacy redaction, fallback handling, and deterministic MCP++
receipts.

## Fallback Behavior

| Condition | Required behavior |
| --- | --- |
| Unsupported hardware | Return structured `unsupported` or `display_capability_missing`; do not emit raw camera, microphone, audio, display, Meta Neural Band, captouch, motion, GPS, IPFS, libp2p, or MCP++ payload claims. Route to mobile-card, display-webapp, simulator, or audio-summary fallback when policy allows. |
| Missing DAT credentials or package access | Keep native DAT disabled, report `package_or_release_channel_unavailable` or `dat_sdk_unlinked`, and preserve bridge-only mocks plus Web App/mobile fallback. |
| Missing display access | Report `display_sdk_unlinked`, `dam_disabled`, `target_required`, `firmware_update_required`, `dat_app_update_required`, or `display_lifecycle_error` with a reset or rollback action. |
| Denied permissions | Emit a policy-denial receipt and stop the operation before camera capture, microphone capture, playback, display render, motion/GPS, Meta Neural Band, or captouch data leaves the adapter. |
| Unavailable audio routes | Report `route_lost`, `route_unavailable`, or `degraded`; avoid raw-audio leakage; fall back to phone speaker, text/mobile-card, or no-audio mode according to policy. |
